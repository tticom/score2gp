# The Pitch Range of the Standard Guitar on the Treble Stave

## Introduction to Guitar Transposition
The guitar is scored exclusively using the treble clef (also known as the G clef). However, to avoid an impractical number of ledger lines, the guitar is treated as an octave-transposing instrument. Music for the guitar is written exactly one octave higher than it actually sounds. For instance, middle C (C4 in scientific pitch notation) is written in the third space of the treble stave, but when played on the guitar, it sounds as C3.

## The Treble Stave and Ledger Lines
The standard musical stave consists of five lines and four spaces. Notes falling outside this primary boundary are accommodated using short, horizontal extensions known as ledger lines.

### The Lowest Register (Below the Stave)
The lowest natural pitch on a guitar in standard tuning (E-A-D-G-B-E) is the open sixth string, E.
* **Sounding Pitch:** E2
* **Written Pitch:** E3
* **Visual Representation:** This note is positioned below three distinct ledger lines beneath the primary stave.
* The notes ascending from this low E (F, G, A, B, C) occupy the subsequent spaces and ledger lines until reaching D, which sits in the space directly below the first line of the stave (the open fourth string).

### The Primary Stave Range
The notes that fall entirely within the standard five lines of the treble stave encompass the middle register of the guitar.
* **Lines (Ascending):** E, G, B, D, F
* **Spaces (Ascending):** F, A, C, E
* This section roughly covers the range from the fretted notes on the fourth string up to the first few frets of the first string.

### The Upper Register (Above the Stave)
As the guitarist ascends the fretboard on the higher strings, ledger lines above the stave become strictly necessary.
* The high E (the open first string) is written in the top space of the stave.
* Notes extending beyond the 12th fret on the first string rely entirely on stacked ledger lines.
* **Highest Standard Pitch:** A modern guitar featuring 24 frets reaches a high E.
* **Sounding Pitch:** E5
* **Written Pitch:** E6
* **Visual Representation:** This practical maximum limit is written squarely on the fourth ledger line above the stave.

## Open Strings on the Stave
For absolute clarity, the open strings of the standard guitar are represented on the written stave as follows:
1. **6th String (E):** Below three ledger lines beneath the stave.
2. **5th String (A):** On the second ledger line beneath the stave.
3. **4th String (D):** In the space immediately below the first line of the stave.
4. **3rd String (G):** On the second line from the bottom of the stave.
5. **2nd String (B):** On the third (middle) line of the stave.
6. **1st String (E):** In the fourth (top) space of the stave.
